# Curious Behaviors

Rabbits have instinctive behaviors that may seem strange but are completely normal.

---

## Circling
- Goes around your feet in circles.
- Usually **courtship** or excitement.
- Very common in unspayed/unneutered rabbits.

---

## Chinning (Rubbing Chin)
- Rubs chin against objects or people.
- Is **marking territory** with their scent glands.
- Means: "this is mine."

---

## Digging
- Scratches at blankets, carpets, or their bed.
- **Natural instinctive** behavior.
- Provide dig boxes to satisfy this need.

---

## Throwing Objects
- Tosses their food bowl or toys.
- Can be play or frustration.
- Sometimes seeking attention.

---

## Spraying (Urine Marking)
- Sprays urine vertically.
- Marks territory (more common in unneutered males).
- Spaying/neutering usually eliminates it.

---

## Ignoring / Turning Their Back
- Moves away from you and deliberately turns their back.
- Means **anger or disapproval**.
- Can happen if you've bothered them, picked them up against their will, or changed something in their space.
- Give them time and offer a treat to make amends.

---

## Quick Guide to Ears

| Position | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Upright facing forward | Alert / curious |
| Relaxed to the sides | Calm |
| Flattened backward | Fear / submission |
| Backward + tense body | Anger / aggression |
| One up, one down | Relaxed / half-attentive |

---

## Quick Guide to Sounds

| Sound | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Soft teeth grinding | Content (like purring) |
| Loud teeth grinding | Pain |
| Growling | Anger / warning |
| High-pitched scream | Extreme pain or fear |
| Humming/buzzing | Courtship |
| Snorting | Annoyance or displeasure |

---

## Tips to Understand Them Better

- **Observe the context** → the same gesture can mean different things.
- **Know their personality** → every rabbit is unique.
- **Respect their signals** → if they say "no," listen.
- **Be patient** → trust is earned over time.
