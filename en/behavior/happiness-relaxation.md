# Signs of Happiness and Relaxation

Learning to recognize when your rabbit is happy and relaxed will help you understand their emotional well-being.

---

## Signs of Happiness

### Binky
The famous **"binky"** is the joy jump:
- Jumps in the air with all 4 paws.
- Twists or turns their body mid-air.
- Runs at full speed afterwards.

This is the ultimate expression of joy. Your rabbit is delighted!

### Flopping (Lying on Side)
- Suddenly drops onto their side.
- Looks "dead" but is very relaxed.
- Indicates they feel **completely safe**.

### Purring / Soft Teeth Grinding
- Softly grinds teeth while being petted.
- Equivalent to a cat's purr.
- Means: "I'm very comfortable."

### Licking
- Licks your hands or clothes.
- Shows **affection and trust**.
- Means they consider you part of their group.

### Active Nose
- Moves nose constantly.
- Indicates curiosity and well-being.
- A still nose can indicate stress or illness.

---

## Signs of Relaxation

### Loaf Position
- Sitting with legs tucked underneath.
- Looks like a loaf of bread.
- Relaxed but alert.

### Fully Stretched Out
- Lying with legs extended.
- Sometimes on their back (only if they trust a lot).
- Maximum relaxation.

### Half-Closed Eyes
- Eyelids half-closed.
- Drowsy or very calm.

### Relaxed Ears
- Ears in natural position.
- Not backward or very erect.
- Indicates calmness.
