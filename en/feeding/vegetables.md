# Vegetable Guide for Your Rabbit

Fresh vegetables are an essential complement in your rabbit's diet. They provide hydration, vitamins, and nutritional variety.

---

## How Much Should They Eat?

- **Amount:** Minimum 2 cups of vegetables per 3 kg (6.5 lbs) of body weight per day.
- **Variety:** At least **3 different types** of leafy greens daily.
- **Proportion:** Approximately **10-15%** of their total diet.

---

## Safe Vegetables for Daily Consumption

These vegetables can be offered every day without problems:

### Leafy Greens (Base of Vegetables)
| Vegetable | Benefits |
|-----------|----------|
| **Romaine lettuce** | Hydrating and crunchy |
| **Escarole** | Rich in fiber |
| **Lamb's lettuce** | Soft and nutritious |
| **Arugula** | Slightly spicy, very nutritious |
| **Carrot tops** | They love them! |
| **Chard** | Rich in vitamins (in moderation) |
| **Spinach** | Occasionally (high in oxalates) |

### Aromatic Herbs
| Herb | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Parsley** | Favorite of many rabbits |
| **Cilantro** | Very aromatic |
| **Basil** | Fresh and digestive |
| **Mint** | Refreshing, aids digestion |
| **Dill** | Soft and aromatic |
| **Oregano** | In moderation |

## Vegetables in Moderation

These vegetables are safe but should be given **2-3 times per week** maximum:

| Vegetable | Reason for Moderation |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Broccoli** | Can cause gas |
| **Cauliflower** | Can cause gas |
| **Cabbage** | Can cause gas |
| **Spinach** | High in oxalates and calcium |
| **Chard** | High in oxalates |
| **Kale** | High in calcium |
| **Bell pepper** | Only the flesh, no seeds |
| **Celery** | Cut in small pieces (strings) |
| **Cucumber** | Very watery, low fiber |

---

## Forbidden Vegetables

**NEVER** give these vegetables to your rabbit:

| Vegetable | Why It's Dangerous |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Potato** | Contains solanine (toxic) |
| **Sweet potato** | High in starch |
| **Onion** | Toxic, damages red blood cells |
| **Garlic** | Toxic |
| **Leek** | Toxic (onion family) |
| **Rhubarb** | Highly toxic |
| **Avocado** | Contains persin (lethal) |
| **Beans/Legumes** | Cause severe gas |
| **Corn** | Cannot digest it |
| **Peas** | Very high in sugar |
| **Iceberg lettuce** | No nutritional value, can cause diarrhea |

## How to Introduce New Vegetables

### Golden Rule: One New Vegetable Every 3 Days

1. **Days 1-3:** Introduce a small amount of the new vegetable.
2. **Observe:** Check their droppings and behavior.
3. **If all goes well:** You can add it to their regular diet.
4. **If there's diarrhea or changes:** Remove that vegetable from their diet.

### For Young Rabbits (< 12 weeks)
- **No vegetables** until 12 weeks of age.
- Their digestive system isn't ready yet.
- Only hay and quality pellets.

---

## Vegetable Preparation

1. **Wash well** all vegetables.
2. **Dry** excess water.
3. **Don't cook** → always raw.
4. **Room temperature** → not straight from the fridge.
5. **Remove** uneaten vegetables within 24 hours.

## Warning Signs

Consult your vet if after introducing vegetables you observe:

- Diarrhea or very soft droppings
- Abdominal bloating
- Loss of appetite
- Lethargy
