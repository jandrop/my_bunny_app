# Guide to Choosing Good Hay for Your Rabbit

Hay is the foundation of a rabbit's diet. It should represent approximately **80%** of their daily food intake. Good hay ensures a healthy digestive system and proper dental wear.

---

## What Should Good Hay Have?

- **Fresh smell**, slightly sweet.
- **Green color**, never dull yellow or brown.
- **Fibrous texture**, with long strands.
- **Low dust**, avoid hay that irritates airways.
- **No moisture**, mold, or strange spots.

---

## Types of Hay

### **Timothy Hay (Phleum pratense)**
The most recommended for adult rabbits.
Ideal fiber, low calcium, perfect digestion.

### **Meadow Hay**
A mix of various plants.
Provides diversity and is usually more aromatic.

### **Oat Hay**
Crunchier texture.
Great for motivating rabbits that don't eat much hay.

### **Fescue Hay**
Resistant and fibrous grass hay.
Good alternative to Timothy, similar in nutrients.

### **Alfalfa Hay**
**NOT recommended for adults.**
Very rich in calcium and protein.
Only useful for baby rabbits (< 6 months), pregnant, or recovering rabbits.

---

## How Much Hay Should They Eat?

- Always **unlimited**.
- Refill their hay rack as many times as needed.
- The rabbit should have constant access, day and night.

---

## How to Store Hay

- In a **dry** and **ventilated** place.
- Avoid hermetically sealed bags.
- Do not expose to moisture or direct sunlight.

---

## Signs of Bad Hay

- Rancid or moldy smell.
- Excessive dust.
- Sticky or damp strands.
- Dull or black colors.
