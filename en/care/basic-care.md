# Basic Care Guide for Your Rabbit

A healthy and happy rabbit needs a proper environment, regular hygiene, and attention to their basic needs. With the right care, your rabbit can live more than **10 years**.

---

## Basic Needs

A rabbit needs:
- **Free-roaming life** without cages.
- **Balanced diet** (80% hay, 15% vegetables, 5% pellets).
- **Daily exercise** in spacious areas.
- **Companionship** (they are very social animals).
- **Annual vet visits**.

---

## Ideal Housing

### Most Important: FREEDOM
- Rabbits **should NOT live in cages**.
- They need to move freely in a safe space.
- If you need to limit an area, use **large enclosures** (never small cages).

### Minimum Space
- Must be able to take at least **3 hops** in any direction.
- Access to an exercise area of at least **3 m²**.
- The more space, the better for their physical and mental health.

### Floor Type
- Avoid wire floors → can cause **pododermatitis** (sore hocks).
- Use rugs, blankets, or solid flooring with substrate.

### Location
- **Indoors** whenever possible.
- Away from drafts and direct sunlight.
- In a quiet area but where they can see you.

---

## Ideal Temperature

| Condition | Temperature |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Ideal** | 16–21°C (61–70°F) |
| **Tolerable** | 10–25°C (50–77°F) |
| **Dangerous** | > 28°C (82°F) (heat stroke) |

### In Summer
- Place **cool tiles** or frozen bottles wrapped in cloth.
- Slightly moisten their ears.
- Ensure good ventilation.

### In Winter
- Provide blankets and warm hideouts.
- Avoid direct heating on them.

## Nail Trimming

Rabbit nails grow constantly and should be trimmed **every 4–6 weeks**.

### How to Do It
1. Use cat nail clippers or rabbit-specific ones.
2. Locate the **quick** → pink area inside the nail.
3. Cut only the white tip, **never the quick**.
4. If it bleeds, apply styptic powder or cornstarch.

### Signs of Long Nails
- Nails that curl.
- Difficulty walking.
- Frequent snagging.

## Hygiene and Grooming

### Never Bathe Your Rabbit
- Cold water can cause **hypothermia**.
- The stress of bathing can be **fatal**.
- Rabbits clean themselves, like cats.

### Brushing
- Brush **1–2 times per week**.
- During molting, brush **daily**.
- Use a soft brush or rubber grooming glove.

### Ear Cleaning
- Check weekly.
- Clean only the visible part with a damp cloth.
- If there's dark wax or bad smell → visit the vet.

### Scent Gland Cleaning
- Located next to the anus.
- Can accumulate brown secretion.
- Gently clean with a damp cotton swab if needed.

---

## Dental Health

Rabbit teeth grow **continuously** (2–3 mm per week).

### How to Keep Them Healthy
- **Unlimited hay** → natural wear comes from chewing fiber.
- Untreated wood toys.
- Avoid soft diets.

### Signs of Dental Problems
- Excessive drooling.
- Loss of appetite.
- Lumps on the jaw.
- Difficulty eating hay.

---

## Daily Exercise

A rabbit needs **at least 3–4 hours** of exercise in spacious areas per day.

### Benefits
- Prevents obesity.
- Improves digestion.
- Reduces boredom and destructive behaviors.
- Strengthens muscles and bones.

### Safe Zone
- Protect electrical cords.
- Remove toxic plants.
- Block access to dangerous areas.
- Watch that they don't chew inappropriate materials (laminated cardboard, synthetic fabrics).
