# Enrichment and Toys Guide for Your Rabbit

Environmental enrichment is essential for your rabbit's mental and physical health. A bored rabbit can develop destructive behaviors and health problems.

---

## Why Is It Important?

Rabbits need stimulation because:
- They are **very intelligent** and curious animals.
- In the wild, they spend hours foraging and exploring.
- Boredom causes **stress, obesity, and destructive behaviors**.
- They need to wear down their constantly growing teeth.

---

## Chew Toys (Essential)

Rabbit teeth grow **2-3 mm per week**. They need to chew to wear them down.

### Safe Options

| Toy | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| **Tree branches** | Apple, willow, hazelnut (dried 1 month) |
| **Untreated wood** | Pine or poplar blocks |
| **Natural wicker** | Unpainted baskets and balls |
| **Loofah/Vegetable sponge** | Natural and safe |
| **Bird wood toys** | Without small bells |
| **Pressed hay sticks** | Nutritious and entertaining |
| **Dried pine cones** | Well dried |

### Avoid
- Treated or painted wood
- Cedar or redwood (toxic)
- Hard plastic
- Objects with small parts

---

## Physical Activity Toys

### Tunnels
- Fabric or cardboard tunnels
- Large plastic drainage tubes
- Connected cardboard boxes

### Platforms and Stairs
- Wooden steps
- Stacked boxes
- Ramps with non-slip carpet

### For Jumping and Exploring
- Low obstacles
- Hoops for jumping
- Homemade agility courses

---

## Intelligence Toys

### Food Dispensers
| Type | How It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **Dispensing balls** | Rolls to release hay/treats |
| **Snuffle mats** | Hide treats in fabric folds |
| **Digging boxes** | Bury treats in paper/hay |
| **Stuffed rolls** | Cardboard with hay inside |

### Puzzles
- Stack cups with treat underneath
- Hide food in small boxes
- Toys that require moving pieces

## Toy Rotation

The key to enrichment is **novelty**.

### Rotation Plan
1. Divide toys into **3-4 groups**.
2. **Rotate weekly** → introduce a different group.
3. "Old" toys will seem new again.
4. **Reorganize the space** monthly.

---

## Environmental Enrichment

### Digging Zone
- Large box with soil, sand, or shredded paper
- Satisfies their natural instinct to dig
- Protects your carpets and furniture

### Observation Zone
- Elevated platform for watching
- Gives them a sense of security
- Can be a sturdy box or low shelf

### Hideouts
- Wooden or cardboard houses
- Fabric tunnels
- Blankets over furniture (caves)

---

## Recommended Play Time

| Activity | Minimum Time |
|----------|--------------|
| Free exercise in spacious areas | 3-4 hours/day |
| Interactive play with you | 15-30 min/day |
| Access to chew toys | 24/7 |
| Toy change/rotation | Weekly |

---

## Enrichment by Behavior Type

### If your rabbit digs a lot
→ Provide a dig box with paper/soil

### If your rabbit chews cables
→ More chew toys + protect cables

### If your rabbit is lethargic
→ More variety, food toys, companionship

### If your rabbit is destructive
→ Redirect to appropriate toys, more exercise

---

## Recommended Commercial Toys

### Good
- Wicker balls
- Hay tunnels
- Natural wood toys
- Woven grass mats
- Hay dispensers

### Avoid
- Exercise wheels (damage their back)
- Plastic balls to roll inside
- Harnesses/leashes (stressful)
- Toys with small parts
- Bells they could swallow

---

## Signs They Need More Enrichment

- Chews cage bars
- Destroys furniture or carpets
- Is lethargic or sleeps too much
- Aggressive behavior
- Overweight from lack of activity
- Pulls out their own fur

---

## The Best Enrichment: YOU

> Your company and attention are the best enrichment. Sit on the floor and let your rabbit explore, play, and approach you.

A rabbit that trusts you will seek your company to play, receive pets, and simply be near you.
