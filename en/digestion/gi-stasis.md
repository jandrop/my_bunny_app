# GI Stasis (Gastrointestinal Stasis)

GI stasis is a **veterinary emergency** and one of the most common causes of death in rabbits.

---

## What Is It?

The digestive tract **slows down or stops** completely.

---

## Causes

- **Poor diet** → little hay, too much pellets or sugar.
- **Stress** → environment changes, loneliness.
- **Pain** → from another underlying illness.
- **Dehydration**.
- **Lack of exercise**.
- **Hairballs** → they cannot vomit.

---

## Symptoms

| Symptom | Urgency |
|---------|---------|
| Not eating | Critical if > 8 hours |
| No droppings | Critical if > 8 hours |
| Very small droppings | Monitor |
| Swollen or hard abdomen | Emergency |
| Hunched posture | Pain |
| Teeth grinding | Intense pain |
| Lethargy | Critical |

### Golden Rule
> A rabbit that doesn't eat or defecate for **more than 8 hours** needs **urgent** veterinary attention.

---

## What to Do in an Emergency

### If You Suspect GI Stasis:
1. **Don't wait** → every hour counts.
2. **Contact the vet** specialized in exotic animals.
3. **Keep the rabbit warm** (no direct heat).
4. **Offer water** with a syringe if not drinking.
5. **Gentle abdominal massage** can help (only if no blockage).

### Common Veterinary Treatment
- Subcutaneous fluids.
- Motility medication (metoclopramide, cisapride).
- Pain relief.
- Syringe feeding (Critical Care).
